# Sprint 3 Summary  
**Project:** AI Agent Concept Workshop & Academic Automation PoC  
**Team:** Sent from my AI  
**Sprint Duration:** 1 May - 14 May  

---

## 1. Overview

Sprint 3 focused on refining the PoC system, expanding testing, improving workshop materials, and preparing for the final stages of the project. Building on the MVP delivered in Sprint 2, the team focused on improving overall system stability, integrating the classification workflow, and strengthening the alignment between the workshop and the live PoC demonstration.

A major focus of this sprint was also preparing for multiple demonstrations, including the client review of PoC v2 and workshop v2, as well as an internal practice presentation with the convenor.

---

## 2. Sprint Goals vs Outcome

| Goal | Outcome |
|------|--------|
| Implement and refine classification workflow | Mostly achieved – classification integrated and tested |
| Improve AI response quality and stability | Achieved with noticeable improvements |
| Expand testing coverage | Achieved at a broader level, ongoing refinement required |
| Rework workshop into guided instructional format | Achieved |
| Prepare and deliver PoC v2 + Workshop v2 demonstrations | Successfully completed |

---

## 3. Work Completed

During this sprint, the team completed the following:

- Integrated the email classification workflow into the overall processing pipeline  
- Improved AI response quality through additional prompt refinement and response validation  
- Expanded testing coverage, including integration testing, edge cases, and performance validation  
- Added additional error handling and input validation to improve overall system reliability  
- Continued refinement between the Outlook add-in, OpenClaw gateway, and Ollama integration  
- Reworked the workshop structure from a presentation format into a more guided PDF/lab-style format  
- Improved workshop clarity and accessibility for beginner-level participants  
- Developed more detailed hands-on exercises aligned with the actual PoC system  
- Conducted internal rehearsal of the final presentation with the convenor and received positive feedback  
- Demonstrated PoC v2 and Workshop v2 to the client and received positive feedback on overall progress and direction  
- Began finalising setup instructions, supporting documentation, and testing evidence  

---

## 4. Work Not Completed

Although significant progress was made, several tasks were still ongoing at the conclusion of Sprint 3:

- Final testing and debugging were still being completed  
- Some workshop and documentation materials still required polishing and formatting improvements  
- User setup instructions and video tutorial were still in progress  
- Minor refinements to response formatting and classification consistency were still being adjusted  
- Final packaging and handover deliverables had not yet been completed  

---

## 5. Challenges & Blockers

Several challenges continued throughout Sprint 3:

- **Balancing Refinement vs Stability**  
  As more features and refinements were added, maintaining overall system stability became increasingly important. The team prioritised stability and demonstration readiness over adding additional functionality.

- **Workshop Redevelopment**  
  Reworking the workshop into a more structured instructional format required significant revisions to both content and delivery approach, particularly to better suit beginner-level participants.

- **Testing Scope Expansion**  
  As the system became more complete, testing requirements expanded considerably, including edge cases, performance checks, and validation of AI outputs.

- **Documentation Requirements**  
  A large amount of supporting documentation was required alongside development, including setup instructions, testing evidence, workshop guidance, and technical explanations.

- **Concurrent Assessment Workload**  
  As the trimester progressed, balancing this project alongside major submissions and exams from other courses became increasingly challenging for team members.

---

## 6. Client Feedback & Presentation Outcomes

During Sprint 3, the team conducted demonstrations of both the updated PoC system and revised workshop materials.

The client responded positively to:
- The improved workshop structure and level of detail  
- The alignment between the workshop activities and the live PoC system  
- The increased stability and refinement of the system workflow  
- The inclusion of more practical, guided learning activities  

The team also conducted a practice presentation with the convenor and received constructive feedback regarding:
- Presentation flow and transitions  
- Reducing text-heavy slides  
- Increasing focus on demonstrating value and project journey rather than only technical implementation  

Overall, feedback received during Sprint 3 required only minor refinements, with most remaining work focused on documentation, polishing, and final delivery preparation.

---

## 7. Key Adjustments for Sprint 4

Based on Sprint 3 outcomes, the team identified the following priorities for Sprint 4:

- Focus on final testing, debugging, and system stability  
- Finalise setup instructions, user manual, and supporting documentation  
- Complete video tutorial and workshop supporting materials  
- Prepare final client handover presentation and demonstration  
- Compile governance, QA, and project management evidence for final submission  
- Prioritise polish, packaging, and professionalism over new feature development  

These adjustments shifted the project into its final handover and delivery phase.

---

## 8. Outcome

Sprint 3 resulted in a significantly more refined and stable version of both the PoC system and workshop materials. The team successfully delivered PoC v2 and Workshop v2 demonstrations, while also improving the overall quality, structure, and usability of the project deliverables.

Although some documentation and final polishing tasks remained ongoing, the project reached a strong handover-ready state by the conclusion of the sprint.

Overall, Sprint 3 marked the transition from active development into final refinement, presentation preparation, and delivery planning.
